About the Program

Comprehensive Care from
Birth to 18 Years.

Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) is a pioneering initiative for early identification of health conditions and linking them for medical and surgical management, providing holistic care for every child in the community.

Focus Area

4 'D's

Screening Process

  • i. CNS: Comprehensive newborn screening performed at delivery points to screen for birth defects in newborns.
  • ii. Community Screening: Conducted by Mobile Health Teams (MHT) in Anganwadis and Government schools.

Management & Referral

  • DEIC: Comprehensive management and care under one roof at District Early Intervention Centers.
  • Tertiary Care: Specialized surgical management and advanced interventions like Cochlear implants.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the RBSK screening and treatment process.

RBSK covers children from birth to 18 years of age. This includes newborns screened at health facilities, children aged 0-6 years at Anganwadi centers, and school-going children from 6-18 years in Government and Government-aided schools.

The 4 Ds stand for:

  • Defects at Birth: Neural Tube Defects, Congenital Heart Disease, etc.
  • Deficiencies: Anaemia, Vitamin A & D deficiency, etc.
  • Diseases: Skin conditions, Rheumatic Heart Disease, etc.
  • Developmental Delays: Vision/Hearing impairment, Autism, etc.

Yes. All screening, diagnostics, and management services, including major surgeries at tertiary care hospitals (like heart surgeries or cochlear implants), are provided completely free of cost to the children identified with the 32 selected health conditions.

Screening is conducted at various levels: newborns at delivery points/hospitals, children (0-6 yrs) at nearest Anganwadi centers by Mobile Health Teams, and older children (6-18 yrs) at their respective government schools during scheduled team visits.

Still have questions? Contact your nearest District Early Intervention Centre (DEIC).
