Operational Workflow

How RBSK Functions

A systematic process that supports children at every step, from initial screening to comprehensive free treatment.

01. Identification

Children are identified immediately after birth at designated Birth Points or through home visits by ASHA workers. Screening for older children is conducted at schools and Anganwadi centers by specialized Mobile Health Teams (MHT).

02. On-site Screening

Dedicated Mobile Health Teams (MHT)—consisting of two Medical Officers (AYUSH), a Staff Nurse, and a Pharmacist—visit Anganwadis and Schools to perform comprehensive screenings for the **4Ds**.

03. Referral & DEIC

Children exhibiting health conditions are referred to the **District Early Intervention Centre (DEIC)**. Here, a multidisciplinary team (Pediatrician, Psychologist, Physiotherapist) assesses the severity of the condition.

04. Free Intervention

Treatment is completely free! In critical cases, surgeries are performed at tertiary care hospitals free of charge, with the entire cost covered by the National Health Mission.

Where Does Screening Occur?

Birth Points

Immediate health check-ups for every newborn at public health facilities.

Anganwadi Centers

Bi-annual screenings for children aged 0 to 6 years conducted by dedicated teams.

Government Schools

Annual comprehensive health audits for all students aged 6 to 18 years.
