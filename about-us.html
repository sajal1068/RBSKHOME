Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK) is a systemic approach to early identification and management of 32 health conditions in children from birth to 18 years.

What is RBSK?

Launched under the National Health Mission (NHM), RBSK is a pioneering initiative that aims at early detection and early intervention for children. The program recognizes that the first few years of a child’s life are critical for their long-term health and development.

"Early identification leads to early intervention, preventing lifelong disabilities and reducing the financial burden on families."

How We Operate

Newborn Screening

Facility-based screening at public health institutions and home-based screening by ASHA workers.

Mobile Health Teams (MHT)

Dedicated teams visit Anganwadis (twice a year) and Schools (once a year) to screen every child.

Referral & Management

Children identified with health conditions are referred to District Early Intervention Centres (DEIC) or tertiary hospitals for Free Treatment.

Our Mission

To improve the overall quality of life of children through early identification of health conditions and providing comprehensive care from birth to 18 years, ensuring no child is left behind due to preventable health issues.

  • Universal Screening
  • Zero-Cost Medical Intervention

Our Vision

To build a Healthy and Resilient India where every child, regardless of their socio-economic background, has access to timely healthcare services, allowing them to achieve their full developmental potential.
