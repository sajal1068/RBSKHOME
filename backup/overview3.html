The 4Ds Framework

A structured medical screening protocol to identify 32 common health conditions in children for early detection and free intervention.

What is the 4Ds Framework?

The 4Ds framework is the backbone of the Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK). It categorizes childhood health issues into four critical domains to ensure that no child suffers from preventable or treatable conditions. By screening children from birth to 18 years, RBSK aims to bridge the gap between diagnosis and treatment.

Screening

Identification of symptoms by Mobile Health Teams in schools/AWCs.

Validation

Confirmed diagnosis at District Early Intervention Centres (DEIC).

Treatment

Free medical or surgical intervention at government hospitals.

D1: Defects
at Birth

Visible or structural abnormalities identified immediately after birth.

Neural Tube Defects

Brain or spine abnormalities during development.

Cleft Lip & Palate

Openings or splits in the upper lip or roof of the mouth.

Congenital Heart Disease

Heart structural issues present at birth.

Club Foot & Hip Dysplasia

Physical deformities in limbs or joints.

D2: Nutritional
Deficiencies

Gaps in essential nutrients affecting physical and mental growth.

Severe Anaemia

Lack of healthy red blood cells due to iron deficiency.

Vitamin A & D Deficiency

Leading to night blindness (Bitot's spots) and Rickets.

Severe Malnutrition (SAM)

Critical weight-for-height gaps needing urgent care.

Iodine Deficiency (Goiter)

Thyroid issues affecting metabolism and brain development.

D3: Childhood Diseases

  • Skin Conditions (Scabies/Fungal)
  • Rheumatic Heart Disease
  • Reactive Airway Disease (Asthma)
  • Dental Caries (Oral Health)

D4: Dev. Delays

  • Vision & Hearing Impairment
  • Cognitive & Language Delays
  • Autism & Behavioural Disorders
  • Adolescent Health (NCDs) - New Focus

Transforming Lives Through Early Detection

Early intervention can prevent permanent disabilities and allow every child to reach their full potential.
