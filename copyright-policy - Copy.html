National Health Mission

Improving Quality of Life Through Early Intervention.

The Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Government of India, under the National Health Mission, launched the Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)...

RBSK is a unique program aimed at improving the overall quality of life of children, enabling them to achieve their full potential by providing comprehensive care in the community.

The 4 Ds Strategy

Screening children for Defects at birth, Diseases, Deficiencies, and Developmental delays—covering 32 common health conditions.

Children diagnosed with select health conditions are offered early intervention services and follow-up care at the district level, completely free of cost. This reduces the financial burden on families by eliminating out-of-pocket healthcare expenses.

To streamline this process, RBSK partners with the Ministry of Women and Child Development (Anganwadi centres) and the Ministry of Human Resource Development (Schools).

Significantly improving the health and well-being of children across the nation.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the RBSK screening and treatment process.

RBSK covers children from birth to 18 years of age. This includes newborns screened at health facilities, children aged 0-6 years at Anganwadi centers, and school-going children from 6-18 years in Government and Government-aided schools.

The 4 Ds stand for:

  • Defects at Birth: Neural Tube Defects, Congenital Heart Disease, etc.
  • Deficiencies: Anaemia, Vitamin A & D deficiency, etc.
  • Diseases: Skin conditions, Rheumatic Heart Disease, etc.
  • Developmental Delays: Vision/Hearing impairment, Autism, etc.

Yes. All screening, diagnostics, and management services, including major surgeries at tertiary care hospitals (like heart surgeries or cochlear implants), are provided completely free of cost to the children identified with the 32 selected health conditions.

Screening is conducted at various levels: newborns at delivery points/hospitals, children (0-6 yrs) at nearest Anganwadi centers by Mobile Health Teams, and older children (6-18 yrs) at their respective government schools during scheduled team visits.
